Design Principles

The mark is intentionally geometric and restrained. It should feel like a lender with construction literacy, not a residential mortgage template or a generic fintech gradient badge.

01

Perimeter and Protection

The outer G behaves like a protected deal perimeter, framing risk and signaling stability around the capital structure.

02

Structural Spine

The internal F is drawn like a beam or frame member, tying the brand directly back to builders, operators, and business-purpose execution.

03

Forward Opening

The open right-side gesture keeps the mark from feeling closed or static. It suggests speed, access, and the ability to move on opportunity.

Typography

The brand should read clean, modern, and premium. Plus Jakarta Sans remains the core system face, with a restrained editorial serif only when you want more prestige.

Primary UI Face

Plus Jakarta Sans

Use for site navigation, buttons, section headings, forms, cards, and all operational text where speed and clarity matter.

Editorial Accent

Cormorant Garamond

Use sparingly for hero emphasis, closing folders, one-pager headlines, and premium pitch materials. Do not use for UI labels or dense functional content.

Wordmark Behavior

All caps, tight weight, disciplined spacing. It should feel like a capital partner, not a playful startup or a residential broker identity.

Voice and Positioning

The visual identity only works if the written voice matches it. The brand should sound decisive, informed, and fast without reading as hype-driven.

Audience

Experienced investors, builders, rehab operators, land buyers, and business-purpose borrowers who value responsiveness and straight underwriting logic.

Tone

Direct, premium, practical, and market-literate. Never fluffy. Never overpromising. Never generic “dream home” residential language.

Messaging Bias

Lead with speed, structure, clarity, draw experience, leverage logic, and repeatable relationships. Avoid jargon unless it helps the operator move faster.
