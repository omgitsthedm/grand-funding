When Rates Decrease Opportunity Increases

August 2, 2024 Market Analysis

What Happens to Real Estate When Interest Rates Drop?

Why are interest rate cuts coming and what does it mean?

It's been a long time coming, but interest rate cuts are finally on the horizon, starting this September. Fannie Mae and Federal Reserve Chairman Jerome Powell both hint at this, indicating the federal funds rate could play a pivotal role.

How do rate cuts boost the housing market?

A rate cut could breathe new life into the sluggish housing market. While the Fed didn't cut rates in August, there's strong speculation for a September cut, potentially lowering the benchmark rate to 5% from 5.25%. If things go well, we might see more cuts in December, setting the stage for a vibrant 2025 housing market.

Why do rate cuts bring more sellers into the market?

Fannie Mae predicts a slowdown in 2024 and 2025, with growth rates of 6.1% and 3%, respectively. More homes, especially in the Sunbelt, will help ease prices. Lower rates could encourage more sellers to list their homes, creating more opportunities for buyers.

What does a refinancing boom mean for investors?

Lower rates in 2024 will likely boost loan originations, with many homeowners and investors holding off on refinancing until 2025 for even better rates. Fannie Mae forecasts a significant increase in refinancing volumes.

How do Arizona and Southern California benefit from lower rates?

  • Increased Affordability: Lower rates make mortgages more affordable
  • Higher Inventory: More sellers entering the market
  • Economic Growth: Rapidly growing metropolitan areas
  • Investment Opportunities: Attractive for rental properties and fix-and-flip projects

Why use hard money loans when rates are falling?

  • Quick Closing: Known for fast closing times
  • Flexibility: More flexible terms than traditional banks
  • Refinancing Opportunities: Ability to refinance into lower-rate conventional mortgages

What smart moves should investors make when rates drop?

  1. Boost Your Credit - Good credit is crucial to benefit from lower rates
  2. Lock in Fix-and-Flips - Start projects now so they're ready when rates drop
  3. Buy Rentals - Purchase rental properties now and refinance later
  4. Wait to Refinance - If you have significant equity, consider holding off
  5. Renovate Your Home - Increase value now, refinance later at lower rates
