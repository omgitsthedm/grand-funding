How to Qualify for a Hard Money Loan

November 4, 2024 Qualification Guide

In the world of real estate investing, securing necessary funds can be a major hurdle. When traditional lending institutions present roadblocks, hard money loans emerge as a viable solution. The distinctive advantages of hard money loans over conventional mortgages lie in their speed, flexibility, and security.

What is a hard money loan and how does it work?

Hard money loans, also known as private money or bridge loans, serve as a short-term financing option predominantly used by real estate investors. Typically secured by tangible assets, these loans are facilitated by private individuals or firms. The loan term generally spans from six months to two years.

What are the three fundamental conditions for qualifying?

1. Down Payment or Equity

Hard money loans necessitate a larger down payment, typically ranging from 25% to 30% for residential properties and 30% to 40% for commercial properties. Grand Funding permits cross-collateralizing multiple properties, enhancing approval prospects.

2. Financial Capacity

Demonstrating financial resources to cover monthly mortgage payments and property's carrying costs significantly influences loan approval likelihood. Cash reserves serve as an indicator of the borrower's ability to meet payments.

3. Real Estate Investment Experience

Grand Funding often considers the borrower's expertise in real estate endeavors. Seasoned professionals with track records have an advantage, as experience translates to reduced down payment requirements and lower interest rates.

What documentation is required for a hard money loan?

  • Hard Money Loan Application
  • Property Appraisal
  • Purchase and Sale Agreement
  • Proof of Income Documents
  • Deed of Trust and Promissory Note
  • Cost Estimate of Project
  • Identification and Legal Documents
