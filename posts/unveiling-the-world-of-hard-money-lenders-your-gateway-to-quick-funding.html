September 8, 2024 Hard Money Basics

In the realm of real estate investing, securing financing through hard money loans is often the key to unlocking lucrative opportunities, acting as bridge loans for project financing. What exactly is a hard money loan?

Unlike traditional loans, hard money loans are funded by private investors or companies rather than banks or mortgage lenders. They offer quick access to capital with less stringent qualification requirements, though typically with higher interest rates and shorter loan durations.

How are hard money loans secured?

Hard money loans are secured by real estate assets through collateralization. Borrowers offer property as collateral, and lenders assess its value to determine suitability. Once approved, a lien is placed on the property, giving the lender a legal claim until the loan is repaid.

Why choose hard money lenders?

Borrowers turn to hard money lenders for quick funding due to streamlined processes and flexible qualification requirements. Unlike traditional lenders, hard money lenders prioritize property value over credit history, allowing rapid access to capital for time-sensitive real estate projects.
