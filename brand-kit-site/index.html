Typography

The typography should support the same tension as the mark: modern and clean in the primary voice, with just enough editorial lift for elegance when needed.

Primary UI Face

Plus Jakarta Sans

Use for buttons, navigation, cards, forms, and most operational brand communication.

Editorial Accent

Cormorant Garamond

Use sparingly in high-impact headings or premium collateral where a more elegant tone is useful.

Wordmark Tone

Heavy sans, generous spacing, clean alignment. The luxury comes from restraint and proportion, not ornament overload.

Brand Voice

The identity works best when the written voice sounds equally disciplined: confident, clear, market-aware, and never overstyled.

Audience

Investors, builders, operators, and business-purpose borrowers who want decisiveness and credibility.

Tone

Professional, modern, bold, and elegant. Never fluffy. Never generic. Never casual for the sake of sounding trendy.

Usage Bias

Lead with clarity, speed, and structure. Let the visual system add polish while the words stay direct and intelligent.
