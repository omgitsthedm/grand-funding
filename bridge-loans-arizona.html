Arizona Bridge Loan FAQs

Common questions about bridge financing in Arizona

What is a bridge loan in Arizona?

+

An Arizona bridge loan is a short-term, asset-based loan used to close a financing gap — typically while you arrange long-term financing, sell an existing property, or close a time-sensitive deal. Terms typically range from 3 to 18 months, with interest-only payments and no prepayment penalty.

How fast can I get an Arizona bridge loan?

+

Grand Funding provides bridge loan decisions within 24 hours and funds most Arizona bridge loans within 3-5 business days. For clean deals with clear title and a straightforward exit strategy, closings in 48 hours are possible.

What is the maximum LTV for an Arizona bridge loan?

+

Arizona bridge loans go up to 75% LTV based on the current as-is property value. The exact LTV depends on property type, location, and your exit strategy. Call for a specific estimate on your deal.

Are there prepayment penalties on Arizona bridge loans?

+

Most Grand Funding bridge loans have no prepayment penalty. When your conventional financing closes or your property sells, you pay off the bridge without additional fees. This flexibility is built into the product.

What are common uses for Arizona bridge loans?

+

The most common uses are: buying before selling an existing property, closing a deal quickly when conventional lenders can't move in time, competing with cash offers, and gap financing between projects or before a permanent refinance.
