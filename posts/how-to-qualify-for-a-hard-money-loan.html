How to Qualify for a Hard Money Loan

November 4, 2024 Qualification Guide

Securing capital for an investment property can be difficult when a bank moves too slowly or underwrites the deal too narrowly. Asset-based financing gives experienced investors another path when speed, collateral, and exit strategy matter more than a conventional mortgage checklist.

Understanding Asset-Based Lending

This type of financing, often called private money or bridge capital, is commonly used for time-sensitive acquisitions, transitional properties, and renovation projects. Terms are usually short, and the property itself plays a central role in underwriting.

Three Fundamental Conditions

1. Down Payment or Equity

These loans usually require a meaningful equity contribution, often around 25% to 30% for residential assets and 30% to 40% for commercial collateral. Grand Funding can also consider cross-collateral positions when the overall deal supports it.

2. Financial Capacity

Demonstrating financial resources to cover monthly mortgage payments and property's carrying costs significantly influences loan approval likelihood. Cash reserves serve as an indicator of the borrower's ability to meet payments.

3. Real Estate Investment Experience

Grand Funding often considers the borrower's expertise in real estate endeavors. Seasoned professionals with track records have an advantage, as experience translates to reduced down payment requirements and lower interest rates.

Required Documentation

  • Hard Money Loan Application
  • Property Appraisal
  • Purchase and Sale Agreement
  • Proof of Income Documents
  • Deed of Trust and Promissory Note
  • Cost Estimate of Project
  • Identification and Legal Documents
