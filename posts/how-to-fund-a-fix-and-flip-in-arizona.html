How to Fund a Fix-and-Flip in Arizona: The Hard Money Playbook

April 17, 2026Strategy

Arizona's fix-and-flip market moves fast. Properties in Phoenix, Mesa, Scottsdale, and Glendale get multiple offers. Deals go under contract with 10-day close windows. If you're waiting on a bank, you're already behind. This guide breaks down exactly how experienced investors fund Arizona flips — and how to structure your loan so you can close before the competition.

Why Arizona Investors Use Hard Money for Fix-and-Flip

Hard money loans are purpose-built for flips. Banks underwrite based on your income and credit history. Hard money lenders underwrite on the deal — specifically, the After Repair Value (ARV) and the quality of your renovation plan. This matters in Arizona because:

  • The competitive Phoenix metro means sellers often accept offers with faster close timelines
  • Self-employed investors and LLC borrowers routinely hit walls with conventional lenders
  • Bank approval timelines (30-60 days) are too slow for auction purchases, REOs, and distressed deals
  • Interest-only payments keep monthly carrying costs low during the renovation period

How Hard Money Fix-and-Flip Loans Are Structured

The core number is ARV — After Repair Value. This is what the property is worth once renovated and sold. Most hard money lenders in Arizona fund up to 70-90% of ARV, depending on the deal quality and borrower experience. Grand Funding lends up to 90% ARV on strong Arizona flip deals.

Here's how the math looks in practice:

  • Property Purchase Price: $280,000
  • Estimated Rehab Budget: $65,000
  • ARV (comps support): $495,000
  • Loan at 75% ARV: $371,250
  • Net to Borrower After Purchase + Rehab: $26,250 working capital

Most Arizona flip loans are structured as interest-only during the renovation term (typically 6-12 months), with a balloon payment when the property sells.

What Hard Money Lenders Look At in Arizona

Unlike banks, the focus is on the deal — not your tax returns. Here's what actually matters:

1. The ARV and Comparable Sales

Your lender will pull comps. You should too, before you submit. Know the 3-5 closest recent sales within a half mile. If the ARV is shaky, the deal won't get approved at the number you need.

2. Your Rehab Budget

Experienced flippers know that a detailed scope of work accelerates approval. Lenders want to see that you're not guessing on renovation costs. A contractor estimate or itemized budget helps move the process faster and often unlocks better terms.

3. Your Exit Strategy

How are you getting out? Sale within 6 months? Rent and refinance? Lenders want a clear, realistic exit. In Arizona's current market, a 90-120 day sale timeline on a properly priced flip is achievable — your lender knows this too.

4. Equity Position

You don't need to put 30% down like a bank would require. But you do need skin in the game. Most Arizona hard money flip loans require 10-20% from the borrower depending on ARV, experience, and deal quality.

How Fast Can You Close?

With a direct private lender like Grand Funding, most Arizona fix-and-flip loans close in 3-5 business days. Here's what drives that timeline:

  • Day 1: Submit deal basics (property address, purchase price, ARV, rehab budget)
  • Day 1-2: Lender reviews, pulls comps, issues term sheet
  • Day 2-3: Borrower accepts terms, title opened
  • Day 3-5: Title clears, loan docs signed, funds wire

The biggest delays are almost always on the title side — not the lender. An experienced Arizona title company that handles hard money closings regularly can meet the same timeline.

Common Arizona Fix-and-Flip Mistakes (And How to Avoid Them)

Overpaying on the purchase. ARV minus renovation minus profit margin minus loan costs = your max purchase price. Run this number before you make an offer, not after.

Underestimating rehab. Arizona heat affects roofing, HVAC, and stucco more than most markets. Budget a 10-15% contingency on top of your contractor estimate.

Using the wrong lender. Brokers add time and cost. Direct private lenders give you faster answers, less friction, and typically better rates because there's no middleman taking a spread.

Not having your exit staged. In Phoenix's competitive buyer market, staging matters. Deals that sit more than 30 days start to get scrutinized. Price right, stage well, sell fast.
